In the United States, first names can be analyzed in relative numbers by birth year for individuals with a Social Security number. This enables meaningful comparisons of the popularity of different names across the decades.

John was the most common name until 1923. In 1880, every 12th boy was named John. In comparison, in 2020, every 108th girl was named Olivia, making it the most shared name that year.

After a growing trend of similar first names beginning in 1901, American parents have increasingly been choosing more unique first names since the late 1960s.

Some assumptions: During the 1880s, baby names were particularly unique, but subsequent generations experienced a notable decrease in distinctive names until the 1960s. This decline may have been influenced by events like World War I, the Great Depression, and World War II. Additionally, immigration contributed to the rise of unique baby names as diverse cultures and languages introduced a wider variety of names. This period resulted in the Baby Boomers having the least unique names in U.S. history.

In 2023, Liam and Olivia were once again the most frequently given short names in the U.S. The average first name length for babies born in 2023 is just under 6.05 characters. On average, women's names are 0.27 characters longer than men's names.

While there has historically been a trend towards longer first names, they have been getting shorter again since around the 1990s.
