How (un)popular your first name has become over time

How has the popularity of your name developed over the decades? Find out with our interactive tool.

By Patrick Vögeli

In the United States, first names can be analyzed in relative numbers by birth year for individuals with a Social Security number. This enables meaningful comparisons of the popularity of different names across the decades.

So John was the most common name until 1923. In 1880, every 12th boy was named John. In comparison, in 2023, every 104th girl was named Olivia, making it the most shared female name that year.

Popularity of first names in U.S. over time

Chart: Patrick Voegeli; Source: Social Security Administration (.gov)

After a growing trend of similar first names beginning in 1901, American parents have increasingly been choosing more unique first names since the late 1960s.

Some assumptions: During the 1880s, baby names were particularly unique, but subsequent generations experienced a notable decrease in unique names until the 1960s. This decline may have been influenced by events like World War I, the Great Depression, and World War II. This period resulted in the Baby Boomers having the least unique names in U.S. history. Afterwards, immigration contributed to the rise of unique baby names as diverse cultures and languages introduced a wider variety of names.

With Liam and Olivia were once again two short names the most frequently given names in the U.S. in 2023. The average first name length for babies born in 2023 is 6.05 characters. On average, women's names are 0.27 characters longer than men's names. We can see that they follow the same trend over time.

Historically, there has been a trend to longer first names. However, since around the 1990s, names have been getting shorter again. In 2023, the longest and most frequently given male first name was «Muhammadibrahim», with 15 characters, while the longest female name was «Oluwafunmilayo», with 14 characters. The most popular shortest names in 2023 were «Jo» for girls and «Bo» for boys.
